Geographic Shifts in Black-White Interracial Marriages: A Look at Regional Changes Over Time

New data from the U.S. Census Bureau reveals significant regional shifts in Black-White interracial marriages, highlighting growing trends of acceptance and diversity across the U.S.

By Whytni Kernodle | December 21, 2024

Recent findings from the U.S. Census Bureau's 2023 study on Black-White interracial marriages reveal geographic shifts in these unions, showing increased prevalence in the South and Midwest. While the numbers in terms of percentage may still seem small, these unions represent a profound cultural change from the past. Notably, Virginia— fifty-seven years after the landmark Loving v. Virginia decision—now ranks among the highest states for Black-White interracial marriages.

"While interracial marriages remain rare, the shift in regions like Virginia marks a significant turn in America’s social fabric. The data reflects the growing empowerment of Black people, particularly Black women, to make choices that reflect both personal freedom and changing societal norms," says Dr. Cheryl Judice, sociologist at Northwestern University.

The study, spanning from 2007-2021, highlights how areas like Delaware and Maryland now report higher rates of interracial households than before. As Dr. Judice emphasizes, this data speaks volumes about the growing acceptance and empowerment of Black individuals to choose their partners, marking a significant shift from earlier social norms.

Source: U.S. Census Bureau, 2017-2021 ACS.
